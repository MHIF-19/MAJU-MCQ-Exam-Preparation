Topic 2: Local and Global Issues in Community Welfare

1. Poverty, inequality, and social exclusion:
- Poverty is multi-dimensional, encompassing lack of income, resources, and basic capabilities.
- Inequality refers to the unequal distribution of opportunities and assets, widening the gap between rich and poor.
- Social exclusion is the process in which individuals or communities are systematically blocked from rights, opportunities, and resources (such as housing, employment, healthcare, and democratic participation) that are normally available to members of society.

2. Health, education, and housing challenges:
- Health: High burden of communicable and non-communicable diseases, lack of primary healthcare access in rural areas, and high out-of-pocket medical expenses.
- Education: High numbers of out-of-school children, gender disparities in literacy, and poor educational infrastructure and teaching quality.
- Housing: Uncontrolled urbanization leading to informal settlements (slums/katchi abadis), lack of clean drinking water, sanitation facilities, and affordable housing.

3. Global citizenship and SDGs (Sustainable Development Goals):
- Global citizenship is the idea that one's identity transcends geographical or political borders, fostering shared responsibility for global issues.
- The 17 UN SDGs (Agenda 2030) are interconnected global goals addressing poverty, inequality, climate change, peace, and justice. Key SDGs include SDG 1 (No Poverty), SDG 3 (Good Health & Well-being), SDG 4 (Quality Education), and SDG 10 (Reduced Inequalities).

4. Local success stories of community-led change:
- Pakistan has a rich heritage of self-help models. Key examples include:
  - The Orangi Pilot Project (OPP) in Karachi: Formulated by Dr. Akhtar Hameed Khan, demonstrating how low-income communities can fund, design, and maintain their own underground sanitation systems.
  - The Akhuwat Foundation: The world's largest interest-free microfinance program, using local mosques to disburse interest-free loans to eradicate poverty and promote self-reliance.
  - The Edhi Foundation: Providing nationwide ambulance, welfare, and orphan support network solely through local volunteerism and community donations.
