Topic 3: Environmental Citizenship and Sustainability

1. Environmental rights and responsibilities:
- Environmental rights suggest that every human being has a right to a clean, healthy, and sustainable environment.
- Environmental citizenship represents our responsibility to protect the ecosystem. This involves adopting sustainable lifestyles, conserving energy, reducing waste, and holding industries and governments accountable for environmental degradation.

2. Climate change and community responses:
- Pakistan is among the most vulnerable countries to climate change (facing glacier melts, heatwaves, and catastrophic floods), despite contributing less than 1% of global greenhouse gas emissions.
- Community responses include building flood-resilient infrastructure, community-led rainwater harvesting, restoration of local mangroves, and adopting smart agricultural practices (e.g., drip irrigation).

3. Sustainable resource management:
- Moving from a linear economy ("take, make, waste") to a circular economy ("reduce, reuse, recycle") to minimize depletion of natural assets.
- Key areas include:
  - Water conservation: Implementing water-saving technologies, checking water pollution, and constructing small-scale dams.
  - Energy transition: Increasing share of solar, wind, and run-of-the-river hydroelectric power.
  - Waste management: Segregating solid waste, composting organic matter, and eliminating single-use plastics.

4. Green activism and environmental education:
- Mobilizing youth, civil society groups, and educational institutions to raise awareness about ecological degradation.
- Initiatives like tree plantation drives (e.g., Ten Billion Tree Tsunami), beach cleaning projects, and environmental clubs in schools/universities foster a culture of ecological stewardship.
